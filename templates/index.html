Fruit

Fresh fruits picked at their peak, stored properly and consumed raw provide the maximum vitamins, minerals, enzymes and nutrients. For added benefits buy organic fruits where available to feel the real benefits and reduce your intake of any potentially harmful chemicals. Naturally, smoothies are colder, thicker and smoother when they're made with frozen fruits. In fact, combining fresh and frozen fruits in the same drink is ideal. Ideally, one should purchase fresh seasonal fruits then peel, seed or pit the fruit and split in to individual servings and freeze them until required. Alternatively, one can purchase the more expensive and convenient option of commerically frozen fruit (especially in Winter). Preferabally select fruit with no added sugars.

Vegetable

One advantage vegetables has over fruit is that they are practially available year round. There are many similarities between fresh vegetables and fruits. They are packed with vitamins and minerals and best eaten raw shortly after harvesting. To get the most out of your vegetables store them in your refrigerator where they can last up to a week. Where recipes require vegetables to be par-boiled prior to blending, be sure to pay close attention to them to avoid over-cooking them and losing their nutrition. Canned vegetables are a good alternative when certain vegetables are out of season and hold similar nutrtion values to that of cooked vegetables. Like fruit smoothies, users should try to produce their own juices out of vegetables using fresh organic products.
